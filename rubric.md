# Grading Rubric — ML Assignment (Linear & Logistic Regression)

The student answered five short questions based on the course book (Linear Regression &
Logistic Regression chapters). Grade the submission **against the book**. Total = **100**.

| Criterion | Marks | What it measures |
|---|--:|---|
| **Correctness** | 40 | Answers are factually correct **according to the book**. Claims that contradict the book earn no credit for that point. |
| **Completeness** | 25 | All five questions answered, each covering the key points the question asks for. |
| **Use of evidence from the book** | 20 | Answers reflect concepts that are actually in the book; **unsupported or invented claims are not rewarded** (and should be noted). |
| **Clarity & structure** | 15 | Clear, well-organised, correctly expressed. |

**Grading guidance**
- Ground every judgement in the retrieved book text. If a claim cannot be supported by the book, do **not** reward it — and flag it in the feedback.
- Produce, per criterion: a score, a one-line justification, and (where possible) the book concept/section it is based on.
- Output an overall total, short feedback, and a list of any flags.
